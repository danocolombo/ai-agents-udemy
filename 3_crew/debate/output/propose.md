Ladies and gentlemen, I stand before you today to propose that there needs to be strict laws to regulate Large Language Models, or LLMs.

Think about it this way — we regulate cars, we regulate medicines, we regulate food. Why? Because they can cause harm if left unchecked. LLMs are no different. In fact, they may be even more powerful and potentially more dangerous than any of those things.

Here is my core argument:

**LLMs are already causing real harm, and without strict laws, it will only get worse.**

First, consider **misinformation**. LLMs can generate convincing, fluent text that sounds completely authoritative — but is entirely false. Without legal accountability, companies have little incentive to ensure their models don't spread dangerous health misinformation, election interference content, or outright lies. People's lives and democracies are at stake.

Second, think about **privacy violations**. LLMs are trained on massive datasets scraped from the internet — including your personal data, your writing, your private conversations — often without your knowledge or consent. Without strict laws, individuals have no legal recourse to protect themselves.

Third, there is the issue of **bias and discrimination**. LLMs have been shown to reproduce harmful stereotypes and discriminatory outputs. If these systems are used in hiring, lending, healthcare, or criminal justice — and they already are — the consequences fall hardest on the most vulnerable in society. Laws can mandate fairness audits and hold companies accountable.

Fourth, **there is no one watching the door right now**. The technology is moving faster than our social institutions. Companies are racing to deploy these systems for profit, not necessarily for safety. History shows us — with tobacco, with social media — that industries do not self-regulate effectively. We cannot wait for catastrophic harm before we act.

Strict laws would not kill innovation. They would *channel* it responsibly. Regulations would require transparency, establish liability, protect citizens, and ensure that this incredibly powerful technology serves humanity — not just the bottom line of a few corporations.

The question is not whether we can afford to regulate LLMs. The question is: **can we afford not to?**

I urge you to support this motion. Thank you.
